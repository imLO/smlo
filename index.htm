PROJECT : HOUSE DESIGN
LOCATION : PEEL STREET, HONG KONG
SEMESTER : BACHELOR YEAR 1 SEMESTER 2
DATE : 2008 - 2009 SUMMER

The site is located at Peel Street, Hong Kong. The footprint of the site is very constricted and bears the distinctive sloped landscape that is characteristic to Hong Kong. The project is to use the analysis from the house study (In this case, Villa Cook By Le Corbusier) and re-interpret the form with appropriate design to the particular site. This would act as a statement protesting to the homogenous residential design that populated over Hong Kong.

PROJECT : URBANN IMPLANT - YOUTH HOSTEL
LOCATION : YEUNG UK ROAD, TSUEN WAN, HONG KONG
SEMESTER : BACHELOR YEAR 2 SEMESTER 2
DATE : 2009 - 2010 SUMMER

The location is in Tsuen Wan, Hong Kong, a relatively old residential district with quite a number of old tenement buildings remain. The design intention is to create an “urban catalyst” which helps boost the community interaction. The youth hostel is designed to integrate with the existing footbridge of the site, which provide an interesting relationship between the footbridge and the plug-in hostel.

PROJECT : PAVILION
LOCATION : NANJING EAST ROAD, SHANGHAI, CHINA
SEMESTER : BACHELOR YEAR 3 SEMESTER 1
DATE : 2009 - 2010 FALL

This semester’s studio was located in Shanghai. In light of the Shanghai Expo 2009, This semester’s project was to explore and develop a modular system for a pavilion that served a particular function. In this project, the geometry and the formation of a screen wall system was developed.

PROJECT : MUSEUM X - MUSEUM OF FOOD SENSATIONS
LOCATION : HARCOURT GARDEN, ADMIRALTY, HONG KONG
SEMESTER : BACHELOR YEAR 3 SEMESTER 2
DATE : 2010 - 2011 SUMMER

Located amidst the highly dense and compact commercial area, the site (Harcourt Garden) ,initially intended to be a public park, was rarely used. A museum is to be designed there to revitalize the area. A material system is developed to create a different museum typology. Museum X is about the integration of light studies, public integration and the articulation of the material system.

PROJECT : PROTEST SQUARE
LOCATION : TAKSIM SQUARE, TURKEY
SEMESTER : MASTER YEAR 1 SEMESTER 1
DATE : 2013 - 2014 FALL

It is a study of the organization, structure and mentality of current protests and occupy movement. A lot of different aspects like makeshift structure, human flow, leadership structure within a camp and landscape of most public squares were investigated. A large landscape structure with continuous terracing and deconstuctiable membrane structure were postulated to see how architectural design could facilitate a peaceful occupying movement and help deter violence.
